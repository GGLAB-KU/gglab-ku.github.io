Our new paper is available on [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/)!

***
Our paper entitled [Are Non-English Papers Reviewed Fairly? Language-of-Study Bias in NLP Peer Reviews]({{ '/assets/pdf/2604.07119v1.pdf' | relative_url }}) is available on [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07119). Across 15,645 peer reviews from major NLP venues, we find that papers focused on non-English languages face up to 40x higher language-of-study bias rates than English-focused work.

We release **LOBSTER**, a human-annotated dataset for language-of-study bias, and show that LLMs can detect this bias with 87.4 Macro F1, making automated screening a realistic step toward fairer peer review.
